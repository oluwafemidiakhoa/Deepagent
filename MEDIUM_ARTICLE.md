**2. Layered Memory: The Mind's Filing System**

Here's where it gets really interesting. The agent doesn't just have "memory"â€”it has a sophisticated three-layer system:

**Episodic Memory**: Like your autobiography, storing significant events and experiences. When the agent encounters a similar problem, it recalls: "Last time I did something like this, approach X worked better than approach Y."

**Working Memory**: Your mental sticky notes. What are you focused on *right now*? What's the current goal? This keeps the agent from getting lost in its own reasoning.

**Tool Memory**: Pattern recognition for actions. Which tools work best? Which tend to fail? What's the typical execution time? The agent learns from every action it takes.

Think of it as the difference between having a cluttered desk where you can't find anything and having a well-organized system where everything has its place.
